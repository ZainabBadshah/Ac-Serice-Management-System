Weak Airflow from the AC

In most cases, it is a sign that your AC's filter is clogged and needs to be cleaned. Filters protect us from dust and other pollutants in the air. However, in the process of removing undesired particles, these filters get stuffy and restrict the airflow of your AC.
  • There is a simple solution to this problem, clean your filters for better airflow. Lack of Cool Air from the AC

  • If your AC is not blowing cool air, it is worth checking a few things before calling in the AC technicians. First up, make sure your AC's temperature setting is correct. If that doesn't solve the issue, take a look at the AC modes. If your AC is set to fan, ventilation, or heat mode, it won't cool your room for obvious reasons. To be sure that a wrong setting is not hampering the cooling, set your AC to the cool mode.
  • If the problem persists, there's a good chance that your AC is running low on coolant gas. It is worth noting that simply topping up the gas may not solve the issue in the long run. Your AC might have a gas leakage issue that requires proper refrigerant repair. So, it is best to opt for Onsitego's AC gas refilling service, which ensures detecting and fixing the leak before refilling the coolant.

AC Blowing Out Warm Air

  • A blast of hot air is the last thing you want from your AC on a summer day. But that's what happens when your indoor unit's evaporator coil gets frozen. Filled with coolant, this coil soaks up the heat and humidity from the air inside your room. If dust builds up on the coil, it doesn't receive the airflow required for its proper functioning. The coil then begins to cool abnormally, which eventually leads to the formation of ice on its surface. This breaks down the cooling cycle and as a result, your AC might blow out hot air.
  • A temporary fix to this issue is to thaw the ice on the condenser. Several new ACs come with dedicated defrost or self-cleaning modes. If your AC unit lacks this feature, run it on the fan mode for half an hour or leave it switched off overnight. If that doesn't take care of the issue, it is time to call the AC service center. Water Leak from the Indoor AC Unit

  • A leaky AC unit can spoil the paint on your wall or even drip water on your electrical items. This issue mostly arises from a clogged drain pipe that's supposed to expel the water out of the AC unit. Due to the delayed drainage through the pipe, the excess water flows back inward and causes spillage. If your AC is quite old, a broken drain pan could also be the reason behind the water leakage.
  • If your AC is new and the drainpipe is in good shape, the water leak may stem from improper installation. In either case, it would be a good idea to book a reliable AC service center like Onsitego's AC repair service in Pune

AC Is Making Unusual Noises

  • You should never ignore unusual noises made by your air conditioning unit. In addition to being unpleasant, these noises serve as an early warning sign that there may be something wrong with your AC. By taking action at this point, you can fix the problems before they get out of hand.
  • A trained AC technician can detect a problem from the noises made by your AC. For instance, hissing sound hints at a coolant leak. Similarly, grinding noise will be most likely be related to the compressor motor. There are more such indicators, but troubleshooting is best left to the professionals. So, next time you hear an unusual noise from your AC unit, call a trusted AC servicing center immediately.

Odour Coming from the AC

  • If your AC is causing an unpleasant smell, you shouldn't mask it with a room freshener. There's a good chance that the odour is caused by an underlying problem. If you notice the smell of a rotten egg, it could be due to a bird's nest or insect stuck in the outdoor unit. If you smell a chemical, you should be alarmed as an AC coolant leak can be a health hazard for you and your family. In this case, you should open every window in the room to keep it ventilated and call for the air conditioning repair service as soon as possible.
